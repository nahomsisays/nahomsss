# RESEARCH_PLAYBOOK.md

## Purpose
This playbook defines the admin research workflow for AI Infrastructure Atlas. The goal is evidence-first classification of AI-relevant facilities while avoiding overclaiming.

## 1) Identifying AI-Relevant Data Centers
A facility can be tracked when there is source-backed signal of AI infrastructure relevance (training, frontier workloads, hyperscale GPU inference, or strategic AI campus role).

Do **not** treat publicity language as confirmation by itself.

## 2) Source Hierarchy (Highest to Lowest)
### Primary sources (preferred evidence)
- Local permits
- Zoning records
- Tax abatement agreements
- Utility filings
- Interconnection queues
- Environmental permits
- Air permits
- Water permits
- Public meeting minutes
- State environmental agency records
- EPA records
- EIA data
- SEC filings
- Court records

### Secondary sources
- Credible investigative/local journalism
- Trade reporting with cited filings

### Lower-trust lead sources (not facts)
- Social posts
- Unsourced commentary
- Promotional copy

Lower-trust sources may generate leads but should not be used as factual anchors.

## 3) Evidence Classification
For each claim, record:
- Evidence for
- Evidence against
- Unknowns
- Verdict
- Confidence
- Explanation

Verdict options:
- supported
- mostly_supported
- partly_supported
- unsupported
- contradicted
- unclear

## 4) Avoiding Overclaiming
- Do not convert possibility into certainty.
- Do not infer final tenant mix from marketing renderings.
- Do not infer definitive water or emissions outcomes from preliminary statements.
- Keep unresolved fields explicitly unknown.

## 5) Facts vs Inference
Use clear labels:
- Confirmed fact: directly supported by high-trust records.
- Strong inference: multiple signals but no direct confirmation.
- Weak inference: suggestive but thin evidence.
- Unknown: insufficient evidence.

## 6) Environmental Claims Review
When reviewing power/water/air/ecology claims:
1. Verify permit status and scope.
2. Verify if values are proposed, approved, or operating.
3. Check for contradictory agency/public records.
4. Record uncertainty if measurements are not public.

## 7) Jobs/Subsidy Claims Review
- Separate permanent jobs from construction jobs.
- Compare public claims vs agreement language.
- Track subsidy magnitude and conditions.
- Estimate public-cost-per-permanent-job only when denominator evidence is credible.

## 8) Uncertainty Recording Rules
Every research record should include:
- Missing fields
- Open questions
- Confidence value
- Last-updated timestamp

Unknowns are a required output, not a failure state.
