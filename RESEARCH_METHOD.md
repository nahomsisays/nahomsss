# RESEARCH_METHOD.md

## Objective
Establish a repeatable, transparent methodology for identifying and evaluating U.S. facilities with potential AI infrastructure significance.

## 1) Inclusion Standard (Tier 2 Only)
A facility may be included only if there is a credible basis for one of these statuses:
- Confirmed AI-relevant
- Likely AI-relevant
- Possibly AI-relevant
- Strategic AI infrastructure campus

If no AI connection is supportable, exclude from MVP.

## 2) Evidence Collection Priorities
Prioritize primary and high-signal sources:
1. Regulatory filings and permits
2. Utility commission records and interconnection materials
3. Planning/zoning and local government documents
4. Company statements and investor materials
5. Reputable investigative/local reporting
6. Geospatial/satellite corroboration where useful

## 3) Claim Decomposition Workflow
For each facility:
1. Break narrative into discrete claims (power, water, jobs, subsidy, AI usage, timeline).
2. Assign each claim a claimant and date when available.
3. Attach one or more sources.
4. Assign claim assessment: supported / unsupported / exaggerated / contradicted / unclear.
5. Assign knowledge status: confirmed_fact / strong_inference / weak_inference / unknown.
6. Write a short assessment note that explains reasoning and uncertainty.

## 4) Confidence Discipline
- **Confirmed fact:** Explicitly evidenced by strong primary records.
- **Strong inference:** Not directly stated, but highly supported by multiple high-quality signals.
- **Weak inference:** Plausible but thin or indirect evidence.
- **Unknown:** No adequate evidence yet.

Never upgrade confidence tier for narrative smoothness.

## 5) Impact Metrics Protocol
### Power
- Capture estimates and ranges in MW where available.
- Separate developer aspiration from contracted/approved capacity.

### Water
- Record MGD estimates/ranges and basis.
- Distinguish operational cooling demand from construction-phase needs.

### Air Pollution / Ecological-Land Risk
- Store qualitative risk notes tied to permits, generator plans, land conversion, habitat sensitivity, or local monitoring concerns.

### Jobs
- Separate construction job claims from permanent operations jobs.
- MVP should emphasize permanent jobs in comparison views.

### Subsidies
- Track tax abatements, grants, infrastructure support, discounted power arrangements, or other public incentives.

## 6) Contradiction Handling
When sources conflict:
- Preserve both claims.
- Mark contradiction explicitly.
- Add a concise adjudication note and current best-supported interpretation.
- Keep unresolved status if evidence remains insufficient.

## 7) Review Cadence
- High-impact records: review at least quarterly.
- Lower-priority records: review semi-annually.
- Critical claims (power, water, subsidy): flag for earlier refresh when new filings appear.

## 8) Transparency Requirements
- Every material facility-level statement should be traceable to source records.
- Unknowns must remain visible to users (not hidden for presentation neatness).
- Method changes should be documented with date and rationale.
